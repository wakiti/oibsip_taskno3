TEMPERATURE CONVERTER

Temperature is a measure of how hot or cold something is.

  • In Celsius, 0 degrees is the freezing point of water, and 100 degrees is the boiling point of water, making it a common scale used worldwide. To convert from Celsius to Fahrenheit: Multiply the temperature in Celsius by 9/5 and then add 32. Fahrenheit = (Celsius × 9/5) + 32

  • In Fahrenheit, 32 degrees is the freezing point of water, and 212 degrees is the boiling point of water, and it is primarily used in the United States and a few other countries. To convert from Fahrenheit to Celsius: Subtract 32 from the temperature in Fahrenheit and then multiply by 5/9. Celsius = (Fahrenheit - 32) × 5/9
